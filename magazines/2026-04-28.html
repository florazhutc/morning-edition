Vim Tool 🤓

Vim-Powered Terminal Spreadsheet Handles CSV, TSV, and Formulas

While speccing out this spreadsheet tool, I realized that I never had to think about the keybindings. It all just came naturally from Vim. Normal/insert/visual modes, hjkl navigation, dd/yy/p, :w, :q. The usual muscle memory works. It supports CSV/TSV import and export, and a native .cell format that preserves formulas. The formula engine handles SUM, AVERAGE, COUNT, MIN, MAX, and IF with range references. The codebase is a Cargo workspace: a pure cell-sheet-core library (no TUI dependency) and a cell-sheet-tui crate on top of ratatui. Early days, but it's usable. To try it out: cargo install

Actionable Insight

This project cleverly targets a niche but highly productive user base: Vim enthusiasts who operate primarily within the terminal. By porting familiar Vim keybindings directly to a spreadsheet, it eliminates the cognitive overhead of learning new shortcuts, significantly enhancing workflow efficiency for data manipulation and quick analysis without leaving the command line environment. Its modular Rust codebase further suggests robustness and potential for extended functionality, making it a promising tool for terminal-native data management.

Community Voice

The community is highly enthusiastic about a terminal-based spreadsheet editor with Vim keybindings, recognizing it as a "badly needed" tool and praising its Rust implementation and architectural choices. While celebrating its core concept, users are keenly requesting crucial features for wider adoption, including support for `xlsx` and OpenDocument formats, more advanced and stable algorithms, robust scripting capabilities (especially programmatic cell access and range passing), and sophisticated spreadsheet functionalities like recalculation and undo/redo. There's a strong desire for it to evolve beyond existing tools like `xan` and `sc-im`.

🚫 Regulatory Action

China Orders Meta to Unwind $2 Billion Manus AI Deal

China blocks Meta's $2 billion takeover of AI startup Manus China's state planner asked Manus and Meta to withdraw the acquisition transaction, following the tech giant announcing a $2 billion takeover plan in December. The deal has garnered scrutiny from both China and Washington, with Beijing launching a probe into the transaction in January. In March, Meta told CNBC that the acquisition "complied fully with applicable law." China's state planner on Monday called for Meta to unwind its $2 billion acquisition of Manus, a Singaporean artificial intelligence startup with Chinese roots.

Actionable Insight

This regulatory block by China on Meta's acquisition of a Singaporean AI startup with Chinese roots underscores the intensifying geopolitical scrutiny on cross-border tech M&A, particularly in sensitive sectors like artificial intelligence. It signals China's assertive stance on controlling tech assets and data, even extraterritorially, and highlights the complex challenges companies face navigating a fragmented global regulatory landscape where national security and economic control often trump market dynamics.

Community Voice

The community largely questions the legal basis and practical enforceability of China's attempt to annul Meta's acquisition of Manus, especially since Manus is a Singapore-based company and the deal is reportedly completed. Many perceive this action as a strong signaling move by Beijing to assert control over PRC-originated technology and capital outflows, rather than a practically reversible transaction, with some speculating about either export or capital controls as underlying motivations.
